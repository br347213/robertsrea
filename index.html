Subject Profile

REF: 1927-2010

Robert S. Rea ("Bob") represents the archetypal Federal Agent of the mid-20th century: educated, disciplined, and highly mobile. Born on a family farm in Vina, Alabama, Subject demonstrated early aptitude, leaving for college at age 16.

Following graduation from the University of Alabama School of Law and service in the U.S. Navy, Subject joined the Federal Bureau of Investigation. His career spanned nearly 30 years as a Special Agent, followed by contract work on background investigations (BICS Unit) that extended his service to the Bureau to roughly 50 years.

Career Trajectory

  • 1947: Marriage to Norma Nelson (Winona, MN).
  • 1950s-60s: Field assignments in Pittsburgh, Newark, Syracuse, and Houston.
  • 1965: Lead Investigator, Brink's Vault Robbery (Syracuse).
  • 1968: Assigned to MURKIN (MLK Assassination) leads in Upstate NY.
  • Post-Bureau: Private contractor for security clearances.

Major Case: Brink's

Evidence Log #1965-OCT

Incident: In October 1965, a criminal gang led by Joel Singer breached the Brink's armored car headquarters in Syracuse, NY.

M.O.: Subject Rea investigated a highly unusual entry method. The perpetrators utilized a 20mm Finnish Lahti anti-tank cannon to fire over 30 armor-piercing rounds into the reinforced concrete vault.

Weapon Specs

Type: Lahti L-39

Origin: Finland

Caliber: 20mm

Use: Anti-Tank

"The Finnish Boombeast"

Agent's Role

SA Robert Rea was instrumental in "cracking" the case. The investigation hinged on tracing the provenance of the heavy artillery used in the heist. The gang escaped with over $400,000 (approx. $4M today). Through dogged pursuit, Rea and the Bureau tracked fugitive Joel Singer to Montreal.

SUBJECT: UNSUB; Assassination of Doctor MARTIN LUTHER KING

Re Birmingham telephone call to Albany 4/5/68...

On 4/5/68 DAVID DALY of the Birmingham Office called and advised the writer that a laundry mark had been found on a T-shirt and a pair of shorts in the hotel room from which it is believed Doctor MARTIN LUTHER KING was assassinated on 4/4/68...

...investigation by Birmingham had disclosed that much of the material found in the room was traceable to Birmingham, therefore, they desired to check on the laundry mark... made by Textile Marking Machine Company, Syracuse, New York.

Mr. DON BROUGHTON, Textile Marking Machine Company, 2204 Erie Blvd., East, Syracuse, New York, advised the writer that the only company in Birmingham, Alabama using tape which was 1/4 inch wide... was Aladdin Cleaners.

SA Robert S. Rea

The "Laundry Mark" Lead

This document connects Subject Rea directly to the forensic breakthrough that identified the assassin.

When the killer fled the rooming house in Memphis, he dropped a bundle containing a rifle and clothes. The FBI found a laundry mark ("2B-6") on the shorts.

Because the machine that made that mark was manufactured in Syracuse, SA Rea was deployed to interview the manufacturer ("Textile Marking Machine Co." on Erie Blvd).

CRITICAL IMPACT: Rea's interview confirmed the specific tape size was used by Aladdin Cleaners in Birmingham, AL. This narrowed the search radius significantly, helping the Bureau trace "Eric Galt" (James Earl Ray's alias) to Alabama.

Civilian Activities

Retirement Phase

The Radio Voice

Following retirement to Lake Havasu City, AZ, Subject utilized his voice not for interrogation, but for ministry. He served as a DJ for a Christian radio station and performed in community productions.

Ministry & Music

A member of the "Sun City MusicMakers" and active in Lutheran church choirs in every city he resided. Known for helping construct St. Andrew Lutheran Church in Las Vegas.

Character Assessment

"Steady, warm, and quietly impressive. A man who chased down bank robbers with anti-tank guns, then spent his golden years singing in the choir."
